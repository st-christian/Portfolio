Splashey - Paper Puzzle

The aim of the game is to colour in as much of the playing area as possible with a paint blob called "Splashey" without being wiped out by the enemy monster sponges. When 80% of the playing area has been coloured in, the level is successfully completed.
The game was developed as a project for the university together with a fellow student.

Master's Thesis: Enhancing Co-Located Synchronous CollaborativeWorking with Asymmetric Augmented Reality Technology.

The thesis demonstrates how a notebook can be enhanced with AR technology for collaborative work. The developed prototype connects three AR devices Tablet(Samsung Galaxy Tab S7), OST HMD(Magic Leap) and a VST HMD(Varjo XR3) together with three notebooks in a local network. Due to the different technologies and operating systems, the prototype has been developed using the cross-platform game engine Unity.

AR-Cube - Augmented Reality Puzzle Game

”AR-Cube” is an augmented reality puzzle game. The goal of the game is to move a player through different rooms to reach his goal. The player is controlled by a marker-based TUI (Tangible User Interfaces). The game was developed using the ”Unity Engine” and for the marker tracking the library ”Vuforia” was used.

ZomBreak [In Progress]

In Zombreak, you play as a survivor who must navigate through a world infested with zombies. The game is turn-based, meaning you have time to strategize your moves carefully. You can search for supplies, craft weapons, and work with other survivors to increase your odds of survival. But beware: every decision you make has consequences, and a single mistake can cost you your life. In each turn, you must decide whether to move, attack, scavenge, or use a special ability. As you progress through the game, you'll encounter more powerful zombies and new challenges that require careful planning and strategy.

Type Adventure

Type Adventure is a simple typing game.
The gameplay consists of words being written above the enemies, which then have to be typed in by the player (on the PC by the keyboard, on Android by an on-screen keyboard). If the word is typed correctly, the opponent dies and you get points based on the length of the word. The game gradually becomes faster, so you have less time to enter the word, but you also get more points.
The game was developed as a project for the university together with a fellow student.
